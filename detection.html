Detection of Adulterated Foods

Simple household methods to check common food adulteration.

  • Water Adulteration: Put a drop of milk on a polished surface. Pure milk flows slowly leaving a white trail; adulterated with water flows quickly without a trace.
  • Starch Adulteration: Add 2 drops of iodine solution. Blue color ‚Üí starch present.
  • Detergent Adulteration: Shake milk. Persistent foam indicates detergent.

  • Water Test: Add a spoon of honey in a glass of water. Pure honey settles at the bottom, adulterated dissolves quickly.
  • Heat Test: Heat a small amount. Pure honey caramelizes but does not foam; adulterated foams strongly.

  • Chicory Adulteration: Sprinkle coffee powder in water. Chicory sinks quickly; pure coffee floats.
  • Starch Test: Add iodine to a coffee solution. Blue color indicates starch adulteration.

  • Argemone Oil: Add a few drops of nitric acid. Reddish-brown color shows adulteration.
  • Freezing Test: Pure oils solidify evenly on cooling. Uneven solidification ‚Üí adulterated.

  • Turmeric (lead chromate): Mix turmeric in dilute hydrochloric acid ‚Üí forms yellow precipitate if adulterated.
  • Red chili powder (brick powder): Mix with water ‚Üí adulterated powder settles quickly.
  • Artificial dyes:Dip in alcohol or water ‚Üí colors dissolve if artificial.

  • Exhausted leaves: Brew tea ‚Üí weak color and taste indicate poor quality.
  • Synthetic colors: Boil in water ‚Üí unnatural color dissolves.
  • Foreign leaves: Examine visually ‚Üí presence of small stems, sticks, or different leaves.

  • Dye: Soak in water ‚Üí Water turns green if dye is present.
  • Chemical colors: Rub on paper ‚Üí Green stains show chemical coloring.

  • Papaya Seeds: Put in water ‚Üí Papaya seeds float; real pepper sinks.

  • Chemicals: Artificially ripened fruits are bright yellow outside but raw inside.
  • Chemicals: Keep in water ‚Üí Such fruits float unnaturally.
